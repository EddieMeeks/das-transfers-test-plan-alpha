Benefits

• You can transfer a maximum of 10% of your apprenticeship levy funds to another business


• Your transfer must cover the full amount of an apprenticeship and not rely on co-investment from the receiving employer


• A transfer can fund apprenticeships up to the maximum funding band of £27,000


• If the cost of the apprenticeship is higher than the maximum funding band, it is the responsibility of the receiving employer to pay the difference


• Once a transfer has been made, it cannot be refunded to you


• If at any point in the apprenticeship you’re no longer able to continue transferring funds, the receiving employer would be asked to make a 10% contribution and the government will pay the remaining 90%

Eligibility

• Any employer is eligible to receive a transfer, provided they are not sending any transfers themselves


• Any levy-paying employer can send a transfer, provided they are not receiving any transfers themselves


• You can only transfer funds to one receiving employers at any time


• There is no maximum number of apprentices your transfer funds can support  


What funds can be used for

• A transfer can only fund apprenticeship standards. Apprenticeship frameworks are not included in the scheme.


• You can find apprenticeship standards here


• Transferred funds can only be used by the receiving employer for the training and assessment of their apprentice


• Wages, equipment and any other costs related to employing an apprentice are not funded by transfers


• If an apprenticeship stops, your transferred payments will also stop
